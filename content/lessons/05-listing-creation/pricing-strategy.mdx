# Pricing Strategy for Resellers

**Objective:** Set prices that win the Buy Box, protect your margin, and avoid the destructive pricing patterns that erode profitability.

Pricing is where most new resellers make costly mistakes. They either price too high (never win the Buy Box) or enter a race to the bottom (win the Buy Box but lose money on every sale). Sustainable pricing requires a margin floor and a competitive ceiling.

## Your Pricing Foundation: The Margin Floor

Before you set a price, calculate your minimum acceptable price — the point below which every sale loses money.

**Margin floor formula:**
```
Minimum Price = (Product Cost + Inbound Shipping Cost + Amazon Fees) / (1 - Target Margin %)
```

Example:
- Product cost: $8.00
- Inbound shipping to FBA: $0.80 (10% of product cost as rough estimate)
- Amazon FBA fee: $3.20
- Referral fee (8%): $1.44 (at $18 price)
- Total cost: $13.44
- Target 20% margin: Minimum price = $13.44 / 0.80 = $16.80

Set this as your floor. You will never price below $16.80 for this product regardless of competitive pressure.

## Competitive Pricing Range

Once you have your floor, look at the current Buy Box price and identify the competitive range:

**Target range:** 0% to 5% above current Buy Box price

If the Buy Box is currently at $18.99, your competitive range is $18.99 to $19.94. Pricing within this range makes you eligible for Buy Box rotation without sacrificing significant margin.

**Do not price below the current Buy Box price on day one.** There is no need — matching the price gets you into rotation, and the FBA advantage will give you Buy Box time. Undercutting signals to other sellers that you are starting a price war.

## Repricing: Manual vs. Automated

**Manual repricing** means logging into Seller Central and adjusting prices yourself. This is acceptable for a small catalog (under 20 SKUs) but becomes unsustainable as your business grows.

**Automated repricing tools** (Repricer Express, BQool, Seller Snap) monitor the Buy Box price and automatically adjust your price to stay within your competitive range. They respect your margin floor — you set a minimum price and the tool never goes below it.

For your first product, manual repricing is fine. Check prices weekly and adjust if the Buy Box price moves significantly.

## The Race to the Bottom

The race to the bottom happens when multiple FBA sellers compete for the Buy Box by undercutting each other by a penny at a time — eventually driving the price below anyone's margin floor.

**How to recognize it:**
- Price has dropped 25%+ over 30–60 days
- Number of FBA sellers has increased rapidly
- Many sellers have zero or very few reviews (new accounts entering for quick profit)

**How to respond:**
- Do not match the bottom price if it violates your margin floor
- Hold your price — if others are selling below margin, they will exit when they run out of inventory
- If the product has permanently deteriorated (the price will not recover), liquidate your remaining inventory at a small loss and redirect capital to a better product

**Prevention is better than response.** Before selecting a product, check its price history using tools like CamelCamelCamel (free). A product whose price has been stable for 12+ months is less prone to price wars than one with significant historical volatility.

## Price History Check

CamelCamelCamel (camelcamelcamel.com) provides free Amazon price history charts. Paste any ASIN and see the price over the past 1–3 years. You are looking for:
- **Consistent price:** Small variation (±10–15%) over time — sign of a stable market
- **Seasonal dips:** Normal — the price drops temporarily and recovers (holiday products)
- **Permanent decline:** Price has been falling for 12+ months and has not recovered — avoid

---

**Takeaway:** Set your margin floor first, then price within 0–5% of the current Buy Box. Never violate your floor. Use price history to avoid products in long-term decline. Hold your price when others race to the bottom — they exit the market eventually.
