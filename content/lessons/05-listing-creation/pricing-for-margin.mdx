# Pricing for Margin

**Objective:** Set your price to be competitive for the Buy Box while protecting your margin.

Pricing is where the model either works or does not. Too high and you are invisible. Too low and you are profitable on paper but losing money after fees. This lesson gives you the exact framework to price correctly.

## The Margin Math

Before you set a price, you need to know your floor — the minimum price at which you make acceptable profit.

**The calculation:**
1. Start with your target selling price
2. Subtract Amazon referral fee (8–15% depending on category, shown in the FBA Revenue Calculator)
3. Subtract FBA fulfillment fee (based on product size and weight, shown in the calculator)
4. Subtract your cost per unit (what you paid the supplier, including freight amortized)
5. The remainder is your net profit per unit

**Target:** 28–40% net margin. If net margin drops below 20%, the product is not viable unless you can reduce your cost.

**Example:**
- Selling price: $28.99
- Referral fee (15%): -$4.35
- FBA fee: -$3.80
- Cost per unit (including freight): -$9.50
- **Net profit: $11.34 → 39% margin**

This product passes. A $2 price drop would not destroy it.

## Finding the Right Price Point

To win Buy Box share on an existing listing, your price should be within 2–5% of the current lowest FBA price. You do not always need to be the lowest. Amazon's Buy Box algorithm weighs price, seller metrics, and fulfillment method — FBA sellers with good metrics can win Buy Box at a slightly higher price than a new FBM seller.

**Practical steps:**
1. Look at the current Buy Box price on the listing
2. Check all FBA seller prices (click "New" under buying options to see all offers)
3. Set your price at or near the median of the current FBA offers — not below the lowest unless you can afford it and maintain margin

## The Two Pricing Mistakes to Avoid

**Mistake 1 — Pricing below your margin floor.**
New sellers often underprice to get their first sale. A sale at negative margin is not a win. Use the FBA Revenue Calculator before you finalize your price. Set a firm floor and never go below it.

**Mistake 2 — Pricing too high and ignoring the Buy Box.**
If you price significantly above the Buy Box, Amazon will not show your offer to most customers. You can be listed and still sell nothing if you are not competitive. Check where the Buy Box is sitting regularly and reprice when the market moves.

## Automated Repricing

Once you have a few products listed, manual repricing becomes impractical. Amazon's built-in "Automate Pricing" tool (free in Seller Central) allows you to set rules:
- "Stay competitive within 2% of the Buy Box price"
- "Never go below $X (your floor)"

Set these rules from the beginning. They protect your margin and keep you competitive without daily intervention.

---

**Takeaway:** Know your margin floor before you set a price. Price within the competitive range for Buy Box eligibility. Automate repricing with a floor price so you never accidentally sell at a loss.
