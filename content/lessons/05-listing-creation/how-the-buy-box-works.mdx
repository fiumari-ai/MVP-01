# How the Buy Box Works

**Objective:** Understand the Buy Box algorithm and learn what factors determine which seller Amazon shows as the default purchase option.

When multiple sellers offer the same product on Amazon, buyers default to clicking one button: "Add to Cart." The seller whose offer appears on that button has won the Buy Box. Studies consistently show that 80–90% of Amazon sales flow through the Buy Box. If you are not winning the Buy Box, you are invisible to most buyers.

## What the Buy Box Is

The Buy Box is the primary purchase widget on every product detail page. It shows the price, fulfillment method, and seller name for the "featured" offer. Other sellers can be found by clicking "Other Sellers on Amazon," but most buyers never do.

On mobile (where the majority of Amazon purchases happen), there is no visible "Other Sellers" link — only the Buy Box offer. This makes Buy Box positioning even more critical.

## How Amazon Decides Who Wins

Amazon does not publish the exact Buy Box algorithm, but years of seller data have identified the primary factors:

**1. Fulfillment Method — Highest Impact**
FBA sellers have a significant structural advantage. Amazon's algorithm strongly prefers FBA listings because:
- Amazon controls the delivery speed and reliability
- Returns are handled by Amazon
- Prime eligibility is automatic

An FBA seller will typically win the Buy Box over an FBM (Fulfilled by Merchant) seller at the same price. This is one of the strongest arguments for using FBA as your default fulfillment method.

**2. Price — High Impact**
Amazon favors competitive pricing. A lower price from an FBA seller will generally win over a higher price from another FBA seller. However, pricing too low relative to your cost destroys margin. The goal is to be within the "competitive range" — typically within 2–5% of the current Buy Box price.

**3. Account Health — Significant Impact**
Sellers with high Order Defect Rates, late shipments, or recent policy violations are less likely to win the Buy Box. Amazon penalizes poor account health even if your price and fulfillment method are competitive.

**4. Inventory Levels — Secondary Impact**
Sellers who are likely to run out of stock soon are deprioritized. Maintaining adequate inventory improves Buy Box eligibility.

**5. Seller Rating — Secondary Impact**
Feedback scores influence Buy Box eligibility. A seller with 98% positive feedback over hundreds of reviews has an advantage over a new seller with no feedback history.

## The Buy Box Rotation

When multiple sellers have similar pricing, FBA status, and account health, Amazon rotates Buy Box placement among them. This means each eligible seller receives a percentage of Buy Box time proportional to their competitiveness. You do not need to be the cheapest — you need to be within range.

## What This Means for Resellers

As a reseller, you are joining an existing listing (ASIN) alongside other sellers. Your offer needs to:
1. Be FBA (use FBA for all first products)
2. Be priced within 2–5% of the current Buy Box price
3. Come from an account in good health
4. Have inventory in stock

When you first join a listing, you may not win the Buy Box immediately — especially if there are established FBA sellers at the same price. Your Buy Box share will increase as you build account history.

---

**Takeaway:** Win the Buy Box by using FBA, pricing competitively (within 2–5% of current Buy Box price), and maintaining account health. 80–90% of sales go through the Buy Box — it is the only metric that matters for conversion.
