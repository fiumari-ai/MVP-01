# Writing Titles and Bullets

**Objective:** Write listing titles and bullet points that rank and convert.

This lesson applies directly when you are creating a new listing (a product not yet on Amazon) or when you have brand registry access to improve an existing listing. Even as a reseller joining existing listings, understanding what makes strong copy helps you evaluate listing quality during product research.

## The Title Formula

Amazon titles serve two functions: they tell the search algorithm what the product is (so it appears in relevant searches), and they tell the customer what they are looking at (so they click).

**The structure:**
`[Brand] + [Product Name] + [Key Attribute 1] + [Key Attribute 2] + [Size/Quantity] + [Color/Variant]`

**Example:**
`Contigo Autoseal Stainless Steel Travel Mug, 16 oz, Insulated Coffee Tumbler with Lid, Gunmetal`

**Rules:**
- Under 200 characters (Amazon's limit)
- Capitalize the first letter of each word
- Do not use ALL CAPS
- Do not include promotional claims ("best seller," "free shipping")
- Include the primary keyword naturally — do not keyword stuff
- Be specific: "stainless steel" beats "metal," "16 oz" beats "large"

## Keyword Research for Titles

Before writing your title, identify your primary keywords. Go to Amazon and start typing your product name. The autocomplete suggestions are real search terms people use. Write down the top 3–5 and incorporate the most relevant ones naturally into your title.

Free tools for keyword research:
- Amazon autocomplete (free, built-in)
- Helium 10 Magnet (free tier, limited monthly searches)
- Google Keyword Planner (free, shows related terms)

## Writing Bullet Points

Bullet points are your conversion tool. Each bullet is a chance to address a customer's question or concern before they ask it.

**The structure for each bullet:**
`ALL CAPS HEADER — Benefit-first sentence. Supporting detail.`

**Example:**
`LEAKPROOF DESIGN — The autoseal lid closes completely between sips so you never deal with spills in your bag or car. Works reliably for 2+ years with proper care.`

**The five bullets should answer:**
1. What is the primary benefit of this product?
2. What problem does it solve?
3. What makes it different from the competitors they are comparing it against?
4. What should they know about size, compatibility, or materials?
5. What does the purchase include? (Package contents, warranty, guarantee)

**Bullet rules:**
- Lead with the benefit, not the feature
- Start with ALL CAPS headers (they help scanners identify what each bullet covers)
- Keep each bullet under 200 characters
- Use plain language — avoid jargon

## A Bad Bullet vs. A Good Bullet

**Bad:** "High quality stainless steel construction with vacuum insulation technology designed for temperature retention performance."

**Good:** `KEEPS DRINKS HOT OR COLD FOR 12 HOURS — Vacuum-insulated walls keep coffee hot and ice water cold all day. No condensation on the outside of the mug.`

The second version is specific, benefit-first, and answers the customer's actual question ("will this actually keep my coffee hot?").

---

**Takeaway:** Title = keywords + clarity. Bullets = benefits + specifics. Lead with what the customer gains, not what the product is made of.
