# Listing Fundamentals

**Objective:** Understand how Amazon product listings work and the two paths available to a reseller.

Before you write a single word of listing copy, you need to understand the structure of Amazon's product catalog and how resellers operate within it.

## How Amazon's Catalog Works

Amazon uses a single listing per product, identified by an ASIN (Amazon Standard Identification Number). When multiple sellers offer the same product, they all list under the same ASIN rather than creating separate pages. This is a critical structural difference from platforms like eBay, where each seller creates a separate listing.

This means: as a reseller, you are almost always **joining an existing listing** — not creating a new one. The product page already exists with a title, images, description, and reviews. You are adding your seller offer to that page and competing for the Buy Box.

## Joining an Existing Listing

This is the reseller path. The process:

1. Search for the product ASIN in Seller Central ("Add a Product")
2. Amazon finds the matching listing
3. You enter your price, condition (new), and fulfillment method
4. Your offer is added to the listing and you enter the Buy Box rotation

You cannot edit the main title, images, or description on a listing you do not own (unless you are the brand). Your job is to price competitively and maintain good account metrics so Amazon's algorithm keeps you in the Buy Box rotation.

## Creating a New Listing

If you are selling a product that does not yet have an Amazon listing (rare for resellers, more common for private label), you create a new ASIN. This requires:
- Full product information (title, bullets, description, images)
- Product UPC or EAN barcode
- Category selection

For most resellers early in their operation, this is not necessary. You will be joining existing listings. The exception is if you source a product from a supplier that is not yet on Amazon — in which case, creating the listing gives you a significant advantage as the first and only seller.

## What Makes a Listing Convert

Even when you are joining an existing listing, the quality of that listing affects your sales. A listing with a compelling title, clear benefit-driven bullets, and high-quality images converts at a higher rate. When you are evaluating products during research, assess the listing quality as part of your analysis.

The key elements of a strong listing:
- **Main image:** Clean white background, product fills 85%+ of frame, high resolution
- **Title:** Clear, keyword-rich, includes size/quantity/color where relevant
- **Bullet points:** Lead with benefits, not just features. Address the customer's question: "why should I buy this?"
- **Description/A+ Content:** More detail, use cases, differentiation
- **Reviews:** Social proof. A product with honest reviews that still has buyers asking questions in the Q&A section is a conversion opportunity for a seller who can answer them.

---

**Takeaway:** Most resellers join existing listings rather than creating new ones. Understand the listing structure before you list your first product, and evaluate listing quality as part of your product research filter.
