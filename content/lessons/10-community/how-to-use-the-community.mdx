# How to Use the Community

**Objective:** Get real value from the community — not just participation, but intelligence you can act on.

The Operator Path community is not a general forum. It is a curated peer network of active Amazon resellers at various stages of operation. The shared posts, supplier finds, and product wins represent real operational intelligence from people running the same model. This lesson shows you how to participate in a way that benefits you and others.

## What to Post

The community has four post types:

**Supplier Find**
Share a supplier discovery — a new contact you have opened, an established supplier you have had good experiences with, or a warning about a supplier who changed their Amazon policies. This is the highest-value post type. Operators who share supplier intelligence build reputation in the community and often receive better information back.

**Product Win**
A first sale, a successful reorder, a margin milestone. These posts build community momentum and give newer operators concrete data points about what real operations look like. Specificity matters — "sold 40 units in 12 days at 34% margin" is more useful than "things are going great."

**Question**
Ask specific, operational questions. The community responds best to questions with context: "I have a product at BSR 22,000 in Home & Kitchen, 8 FBA competitors, price is stable. My sell-through is lower than expected. What would you check first?" That is answerable. "How do I get my first sale?" is too broad.

**General**
Anything operational but not fitting the above — market observations, tool recommendations, process questions, workflow tips.

## What NOT to Post

- Do not post vague motivational content. This community is for operators, not cheerleaders.
- Do not ask for someone else to do your research for you. "What products should I sell?" is not a community question — that is what Product Finder and Module 03 are for.
- Do not share your specific product ASINs publicly if you are currently selling them. Other operators can use that information to compete with you directly.

## How to Extract Intelligence Passively

You do not have to post to get value. Read the community weekly. What supplier categories are others having success in? What problems are operators encountering? What tools and processes are being recommended consistently?

The patterns you notice across 10–20 posts over a month give you:
- Category intelligence (what verticals are active operators currently in?)
- Supplier intelligence (which names come up repeatedly?)
- Problem intelligence (what challenges are common at your stage that you can prepare for?)

## The Reciprocity Principle

Communities that produce value over time are communities where people share, not just consume. If you open a new supplier account, post the category and your experience. If you have a first sale, share the numbers. If you find a market shift worth noting, write it up.

Operators who contribute regularly get more back — in direct replies, in direct messages from other operators, and in the quality of intelligence they receive when they ask questions.

---

**Takeaway:** Post supplier finds and real results. Ask specific, operational questions. Read weekly even when you have nothing to post. The community compounds in value the longer you are part of it.
