# The First Product Decision Framework

**Objective:** Apply a four-filter framework to evaluate any product and decide whether it is worth pursuing as your first inventory buy.

Good product selection is not about finding the perfect product. It is about eliminating bad products quickly and acting on the ones that pass your criteria. The following four-filter framework gives you a repeatable process for every product you evaluate.

## Filter 1 — Proven Demand

**Question:** Does this product have consistent sales velocity?

**Pass criteria:**
- BSR under 10,000 in a major category
- BSR has been consistent over multiple days
- Multiple sellers offer the same product (5 or more)

**Fail criteria:**
- BSR above 30,000
- Only 1–2 sellers with few reviews
- Product is new to market (BSR recently appeared)

If a product fails Filter 1, stop evaluation. No further analysis is needed.

## Filter 2 — Margin Viability

**Question:** Can I source and sell this product profitably after all Amazon fees?

**Pass criteria:**
- Sale price on Amazon: $15 or higher
- Your landed cost (product + shipping to FBA): 40% or less of sale price
- Estimated profit margin after Amazon fees: 15% or higher

**Fail criteria:**
- Sale price under $12 (fees eat the margin)
- Products you cannot source below 50% of the sale price
- Categories with very high referral fees (jewelry, shoes at 20%)

Use Amazon's FBA Revenue Calculator (available free on Seller Central) to input the ASIN and your estimated cost. The calculator shows you exact fee breakdowns.

## Filter 3 — Low Complexity

**Question:** Is this product simple to receive, store, and ship without special handling?

**Pass criteria:**
- Small to medium size (fits in a standard box)
- Does not require special storage conditions (no refrigeration, no hazmat)
- Not fragile enough to have a high breakage rate during FBA handling
- Not a product that requires assembly or instructions

**Fail criteria:**
- Oversized products (Amazon charges significantly higher FBA fees)
- Hazmat, liquids, batteries included in the product
- Very fragile items with high return rates

Complexity adds cost and risk. Your first product should be something that ships easily, stores simply, and rarely breaks.

## Filter 4 — Competition Quality

**Question:** Can I compete effectively with current sellers?

**Pass criteria:**
- At least one competitor has an average listing (room to improve)
- No single seller dominates with 10,000+ reviews and the only winning Buy Box
- The category allows new sellers (some categories are restricted — verify first)

**Fail criteria:**
- The category is dominated by a single private label brand with thousands of reviews
- You cannot source the product at a competitive price versus existing sellers
- The brand is gated (requires approval to sell)

**Note on brand restrictions:** Many brands on Amazon require sellers to apply for approval before listing their products. This is called "brand gating." Check whether the brand you are considering is gated before purchasing inventory. You can check this by attempting to add the ASIN to your inventory in Seller Central — it will prompt you for approval if the brand is restricted.

## Applying the Framework

When you identify a candidate product, run it through all four filters in order. A product must pass all four to be worth pursuing. Most products will fail at Filter 1 or Filter 2. That is expected and useful — failing fast is the goal.

**Example evaluation:**
- Office tape dispenser, BSR #4,200 in Office Products — passes Filter 1
- Sale price $16.99, sourced from a wholesaler at $5.50, FBA fee $3.20, referral fee $2.55, margin ~$5.74 (34%) — passes Filter 2
- Small product, non-hazmat, not fragile — passes Filter 3
- Multiple sellers, brand not gated — passes Filter 4

This product passes all four filters. It is a viable candidate.

---

**Takeaway:** Four filters — Demand, Margin, Complexity, Competition. A product must pass all four. Most products fail at filter one or two. That's the system working as intended.
