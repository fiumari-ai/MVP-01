# Understanding the Best Seller Rank

**Objective:** Decode the Best Seller Rank (BSR) number and use it to estimate how fast a product is actually selling.

Every product on Amazon has a Best Seller Rank. This single number tells you more about a product's commercial viability than any other free data point available to you. Learning to read it correctly is a core skill.

## What BSR Means

The Best Seller Rank is a relative ranking within a category. A BSR of #1 means this product is selling more units per day than any other product in that category. A BSR of #100,000 means 99,999 products are selling faster.

BSR is updated hourly. It reflects recent sales velocity, not total historical sales. A product that had a spike three months ago and stopped selling will show a high (bad) BSR now.

**BSR is category-specific.** A BSR of #5,000 in Kitchen & Dining means something very different from #5,000 in Industrial & Scientific. Kitchen & Dining is enormous — millions of products. Industrial & Scientific is much smaller. A #5,000 rank in Kitchen means significantly higher sales volume than the same rank in a niche category.

## BSR to Sales Volume Estimates

Industry tools (Jungle Scout, Helium 10) use BSR and category size to estimate monthly sales. You do not need these tools for initial product evaluation. Use these rough rules as a starting point:

**For large categories (Kitchen, Sports, Home, Baby, Health):**
- BSR under 1,000: selling 500–2,000+ units/month
- BSR 1,000–5,000: selling 200–500 units/month
- BSR 5,000–10,000: selling 50–200 units/month
- BSR 10,000–50,000: selling 10–50 units/month
- BSR above 50,000: selling fewer than 10 units/month — usually not worth pursuing

**What "good" looks like for a reseller:**
A product with a consistent BSR between 1,000 and 10,000 in a major category has proven daily sales velocity. This means buyers exist, the market is active, and the product is not a seasonal anomaly.

## Where to Find BSR

Open any product page on Amazon. Scroll to the "Product Details" section. You will see a line labeled "Best Sellers Rank" with one or two numbers — the overall Amazon rank and the category-specific rank. The category rank is the one that matters.

If a product has no BSR listed, it has not sold recently or is not eligible for ranking. Do not pursue products with no BSR as a new seller.

## Reading Multiple BSRs

Some products appear in two or three subcategories. Example: a yoga mat might be ranked in both "Sports & Outdoors > Exercise Mats" and "Sports & Outdoors > Yoga." Both rankings reflect sales in those subcategories. If a product ranks well in two relevant subcategories, that confirms demand from multiple buyer segments.

## BSR Fluctuation

BSR fluctuates daily and even hourly. A product with BSR #3,500 today might be #2,800 tomorrow or #4,100 next week. This is normal. What you are looking for is a product that consistently stays within a healthy range — not a specific number on a specific day.

When evaluating a product, check its BSR across multiple visits over several days. If it moves between #2,000 and #6,000 consistently, the product has reliable demand. If it swings from #800 to #25,000 week to week, the demand is volatile.

---

**Takeaway:** BSR measures sales velocity, not total sales history. Under 10,000 in a large category means consistent daily sales. Check it across multiple days before drawing conclusions.
