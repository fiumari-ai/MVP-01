# Your First Product Research Session

**Objective:** Run a structured product research session and arrive at at least one viable candidate.

This is not a theory lesson. This is a protocol. Follow the steps below during your first research session and you will end it with a shortlist of product candidates to evaluate further.

## Before You Start

Install the free browser extensions:
- **Keepa** — shows BSR history and price history directly on Amazon product pages
- **Jungle Scout extension** or **Helium 10 Chrome Extension** (free plans available) — shows estimated monthly sales, BSR trends, and competition data

These tools are not required, but they compress your research time significantly.

## The 60-Minute Research Protocol

**Minutes 0–15 — Pick a category starting point**

Start with a category you either know something about or that has structural advantages for resellers. Categories to consider at the early stage:
- Home & Kitchen
- Sports & Outdoors
- Office Supplies
- Pet Supplies
- Tools & Hardware

Avoid: Electronics (brand-dominated, ungating issues), Grocery (regulatory complexity), Toys (seasonal, gated categories).

Go to Amazon and browse the category's Best Sellers list (find it under "Best Sellers" in the navigation). This is a live list of what is actually selling.

**Minutes 15–35 — Identify candidates**

Scroll through the Best Sellers list. Look for products that:
- Are priced between $15–$60 (sweet spot for margin after fees)
- Do not appear to be a single dominant brand (generic or multi-brand listings are better for resellers)
- Are small and lightweight (lower FBA fees and shipping costs)
- Have 100–500 reviews (established but not over-saturated)

Write down the ASIN (found in the product URL or listing details) for any product that catches your eye. Aim for 10–15 candidates after 20 minutes.

**Minutes 35–55 — Run the filter**

For each candidate, pull up the product page and check:
1. Current BSR (shown at the bottom of the listing page under "Product Information")
2. Keepa graph — price history and BSR trend
3. Number of FBA sellers (click "New" under the buying options)
4. Run the product through the FBA Revenue Calculator with your estimated cost

Use the 5-point filter from the previous lesson. Cross off products that fail any point. By the end of this step, you should have 2–5 candidates remaining.

**Minutes 55–60 — Prioritize**

Of the remaining candidates, identify the one that scores best across all five filters AND that you can source. This is your primary product to research further.

## What a Good Candidate Looks Like

- BSR: 8,000–60,000 in a main category
- Price: $22–$45, stable for 6+ months
- Competing FBA sellers: 5–12
- Estimated net margin (after fees and cost): 28–40%
- Product weight: under 2 lbs
- No brand exclusivity issues

## If You Cannot Find One in the First Session

That is normal. Not every session produces a viable candidate immediately. The protocol gets faster with repetition. Run the session again in a different category. Expect to spend 2–3 sessions across different categories before you land on a strong first product.

---

**Takeaway:** Product research is a repeatable skill, not a one-time event. The protocol above gets faster every time you run it. Your goal for this session: one product candidate that passes all five filters.
