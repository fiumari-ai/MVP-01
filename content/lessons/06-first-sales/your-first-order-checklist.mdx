# Your First Order Checklist

**Objective:** Move from supplier approval to first live listing using this verified checklist.

The first order has the most moving parts. After this, the process becomes a loop. Run through this checklist item by item before you place your order and before you activate your listing.

## Pre-Order Checklist

**Product research confirmed:**
- [ ] Product passes all 5 filter points
- [ ] FBA Revenue Calculator confirms target margin (28%+) at the expected sale price
- [ ] Keepa confirms stable BSR and price history over 6+ months
- [ ] Buy Box competition is manageable (under 15 FBA sellers)

**Supplier confirmed:**
- [ ] Wholesale account approved with at least one supplier
- [ ] Price list reviewed — cost per unit supports margin target
- [ ] Shipping terms confirmed (who pays freight? flat rate or per lb?)
- [ ] Lead time confirmed (how many days from order to delivery?)
- [ ] Supplier allows Amazon sales (check their terms)
- [ ] MAP (Minimum Advertised Price) policy reviewed if applicable

**Amazon account confirmed:**
- [ ] Seller Central account verified and active
- [ ] Tax information submitted
- [ ] Bank account linked for payouts
- [ ] FBA or FBM decision made

## Placing Your First Order

Start small. A first order of 20–40 units is appropriate for most products in the $15–$50 range. You are testing:
- Sell-through speed (how fast does it sell?)
- Supplier reliability (did it arrive as described, on time?)
- Margin accuracy (did the actual fees match what you calculated?)

Do not place a 200-unit order on your first product. Even a product that passes all five filters can underperform. Start small, validate, then scale.

## Shipping to Amazon (FBA Path)

If you are using FBA:
1. In Seller Central, go to **Inventory > Add a Product**, find your ASIN
2. Set quantity and condition, select FBA
3. Go to **Inventory > Send to Amazon**
4. Create a shipping plan — Amazon will tell you which fulfillment center to ship to
5. Label your units (either you apply FNSKU labels or pay Amazon to do it)
6. Ship your box to the assigned fulfillment center
7. Inventory checks in within 1–3 business days. Your listing goes live.

## FBM Path

If you are using Fulfilled by Merchant:
1. Receive inventory at your address
2. In Seller Central, create your listing and set price
3. Activate the listing
4. When an order comes in, you package and ship within your stated handling time
5. Upload tracking number in Seller Central to confirm shipment

FBM is simpler to start. You are in control of inventory. FBA has higher fees per unit but gives you Prime eligibility and removes the shipping burden.

---

**Takeaway:** Use this checklist for every first order on a new product. The discipline of checking each item prevents the most common early-stage errors — wrong costs, unverified supplier terms, and missed account settings.
