# Your First 30 Days

**Objective:** Navigate the critical first month — understand what normal looks like, what requires action, and when to plan your reorder.

The first 30 days of selling validate your research. Some products sell faster than expected; some slower. The goal is to collect accurate data, not to hit a specific revenue number. Here is what to expect and what to do.

## Week 1: Establishing Baseline

In the first week, your primary job is to confirm that the operation is functioning correctly:

- Your offer is in Buy Box rotation (check Manage FBA Inventory — Buy Box percentage should be above 0%)
- Sales are occurring (even 1–2 per day confirms the product is active)
- No customer service issues have emerged
- Account Health metrics remain within acceptable thresholds

If you are getting zero sales after 5–7 days with inventory available:
- Verify your offer is active and FBA
- Confirm your price is within 5% of the Buy Box price
- Check if new FBA sellers have entered the listing and undercut significantly
- Verify the ASIN's current BSR — a sudden spike could indicate a temporary stockout by other sellers inflated the rank

## Week 2: Reading the Velocity

By day 14, you have enough data to estimate your actual sales velocity. Calculate:

**Units sold ÷ Days active = Daily velocity**

Compare this to your original estimate. Three scenarios:

**Scenario A: Selling faster than estimated**
Good problem. Reorder immediately to prevent stockout. Use your actual velocity to calculate reorder quantity: (Daily velocity × Lead time in days) + Safety buffer of 7–10 days.

**Scenario B: Selling on pace**
Continue as planned. Reorder at the halfway point of your current inventory.

**Scenario C: Selling slower than estimated**
Do not panic. Check: Has the Buy Box price dropped significantly since you launched? Have new sellers entered the listing? Is the product approaching a seasonal slow period? One month of data is not enough to declare a product failed — but it is enough to investigate whether something changed.

## Week 3: Reorder Decision

If you are on pace or faster, week 3 is when you place your reorder. This timing accounts for:

- Supplier processing time: 1–3 days
- Inbound shipping to FBA: 3–7 days
- FBA receiving and processing: 3–7 days

Total lead time: 7–17 days. Placing your reorder in week 3 ensures you will not go out of stock before new inventory arrives.

**Stockout cost:** When you go out of stock on FBA, your listing does not disappear — but your FBA offer disappears. You lose Buy Box eligibility during the stockout period, and when you restock, it takes time to rebuild Buy Box share. Avoid stockouts at all costs.

## Week 4: Evaluation and Expansion Planning

At the end of month one, evaluate your product's performance:

| Metric | Target | Your Result |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| Units sold | Initial estimate | _____ |
| Actual margin per unit | ≥ 15% | _____ |
| Buy Box win rate | > 50% | _____ |
| Returns/complaints | < 3% | _____ |
| Account Health | All green | _____ |

If you hit 3 of 5 targets with no major issues, the product is viable. Continue selling and refining.

If the product is working, week 4 is when you start researching your second product. The model scales by adding products, not by increasing ad spend. Each additional product is a new revenue stream.

---

**Takeaway:** First 30 days = data collection. Measure daily velocity by day 14. Reorder in week 3 if selling on pace. Evaluate month-end results against your baseline estimates. Products that hit 3 of 5 targets are worth continuing.
