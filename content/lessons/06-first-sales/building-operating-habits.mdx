# Building Operating Habits

**Objective:** Build the operating routines that keep a multi-product Amazon business running smoothly and efficiently, without requiring your constant attention.

A well-run Amazon resale business does not require 40 hours per week. Most operators managing 10–20 products spend 5–10 hours per week on business operations. That efficiency comes from consistent routines, not from working harder.

## The Weekly Review (60 minutes)

Once per week, run through this sequence:

**1. Inventory Review (20 minutes)**
For each active product, check:
- Current inventory level in Manage FBA Inventory
- Days of inventory remaining at current sales velocity
- Any products approaching 14 days of remaining stock (time to reorder)

**2. Account Health Check (5 minutes)**
Navigate to Performance → Account Health. All metrics should be green. If anything is yellow or red, address it immediately — before the week is over.

**3. Buy Box Review (15 minutes)**
For each product, check:
- Current Buy Box price
- Your current listed price
- Your Buy Box win percentage (available in Business Reports)

Adjust prices if you have drifted outside the competitive range.

**4. Customer Messages (10 minutes)**
Review and respond to any open customer messages. Response time must be under 24 hours — but aim to clear messages within the same day.

**5. Financial Snapshot (10 minutes)**
Review your Payments dashboard in Seller Central. Confirm disbursements are on schedule, your balance is growing, and there are no unexpected deductions.

## The Monthly Review (2 hours)

Once per month, do a deeper analysis:

**Product Performance Report**
Pull a Business Report from Seller Central for the past 30 days. For each product:
- Units ordered
- Ordered product sales
- Unit session percentage (conversion rate)

Products with conversion rates below 5% need attention — either the price is uncompetitive or the listing quality has degraded.

**Profit and Loss Calculation**
For each product:
- Total revenue
- Amazon fees (referral + FBA)
- Inventory cost
- Inbound shipping cost
- Net profit

This is your actual margin. Compare it to your pre-launch estimate. Variance tells you where your model needs adjustment.

**Expansion Planning**
Identify which products are performing well and have remaining market capacity. Plan one new product addition per month once your current products are stable.

## Avoiding Common Time Traps

**Trap 1: Checking sales constantly**
Looking at your Seller Central dashboard every hour is addictive but unproductive. Set a schedule — check once in the morning, once in the evening. No more.

**Trap 2: Reacting to single-day fluctuations**
Sales vary day to day. A slow Tuesday is not a crisis. Look at 7-day and 30-day trends, not individual days.

**Trap 3: Premature product abandonment**
Sellers often kill products after 2–3 slow weeks. Give a product 60–90 days before deciding it is not working. Most variability resolves over time.

**Trap 4: Over-optimizing existing products before adding new ones**
You cannot reprice your way to 3x revenue on a single product. Growth comes from product count. Once a product is stable, move your optimization energy to the next product addition.

## The Operating System in Summary

| Cadence | Activity | Time Required |
|---------|----------|--------------|
| Daily | Check messages | 5–10 min |
| Weekly | Inventory + pricing + account health | 60 min |
| Monthly | Full P&L review + expansion planning | 120 min |
| Per reorder | Place order + create FBA shipment | 30 min |

Total weekly time investment for a stable 5-product operation: roughly 3–5 hours. This is the leverage of the system — Amazon handles fulfillment, customer service, and returns. You handle sourcing, repricing, and reordering.

---

**Takeaway:** Build three routines: daily messages (10 min), weekly review (60 min), monthly P&L (2 hours). Growth comes from adding products, not from optimizing existing ones. The model scales through replication, not through hustle.
