# Going Live Checklist

**Objective:** Confirm every step is complete before your first listing goes live and your first sale can occur.

Going live is not a single action — it is the completion of a sequence. Missing any step creates a gap that will either delay your first sale or create an operational problem after it.

## Pre-Launch Checklist

**Account Setup (must be complete):**
- [ ] Seller Central account approved and active
- [ ] Bank account verified and deposit method set
- [ ] Tax interview completed
- [ ] Two-step verification enabled
- [ ] Return address configured

**Product Selection (must be confirmed):**
- [ ] Product passed all four filters (Demand, Margin, Complexity, Competition)
- [ ] ASIN identified and existing listing quality verified
- [ ] Margin calculated using FBA Revenue Calculator with current Buy Box price
- [ ] Price history checked — no long-term decline

**Inventory (must be received by Amazon):**
- [ ] FBA shipping plan created in Seller Central
- [ ] FNSKU labels applied to all units
- [ ] Inventory shipped and tracking entered in Seller Central
- [ ] Inventory status shows "Available" in Manage FBA Inventory

**Listing (must be active):**
- [ ] Your offer is active on the ASIN
- [ ] Fulfillment method is set to FBA
- [ ] Price is within competitive range of current Buy Box price
- [ ] Condition is set correctly (New)

**Business Baseline:**
- [ ] Account Health dashboard bookmarked
- [ ] Customer messages set to notify via email
- [ ] Weekly review time blocked on calendar

## What Happens After You Go Live

Within 24–72 hours of your inventory being marked "Available," your offer will appear in the Buy Box rotation (assuming you are FBA and competitively priced). Your first sale may come within hours or within several days depending on the product's daily sales velocity.

When your first sale occurs:
- Amazon sends an email notification
- The unit is picked, packed, and shipped by Amazon FBA
- The buyer receives tracking information automatically
- Funds are held in your Seller Central balance (subject to reserve policy for new accounts)

You do not need to do anything when a sale occurs on an FBA listing. The fulfillment is automatic.

## What to Monitor in the First Week

- **Inventory levels:** How quickly are units selling? Reorder before you hit zero — FBA processing takes 3–7 days.
- **Price and Buy Box:** Check daily that your price is still within the competitive range and you are winning Buy Box time.
- **Account Health:** No issues should arise in the first week if your setup was correct, but confirm all metrics remain green.
- **Customer Messages:** Respond to any buyer messages within 24 hours. Response time above 24 hours negatively impacts account health.

---

**Takeaway:** Use the checklist — do not skip steps. Once inventory shows "Available" and your offer is live at a competitive FBA price, you are in the Buy Box rotation. The first sale typically follows within days.
