# Creating Your Amazon Seller Account

**Objective:** Register your Amazon Seller Central account and complete the verification process.

Before you can list a product, you need an active seller account. This lesson walks you through every step — what account type to choose, what documents you'll need, and what happens after you submit.

## Individual vs. Professional

Amazon offers two seller account types:

- **Individual:** No monthly fee, but you pay $0.99 per item sold. No access to bulk listing tools or Buy Box eligibility for most categories. This is not the right starting point.
- **Professional:** $39.99/month flat fee, regardless of how many items you sell. Full access to listing tools, Buy Box eligibility, and category approvals. This is the account type you want.

The Professional plan pays for itself at 41 units sold per month. If you are serious about this model, start with Professional.

## What You'll Need Before You Start

Have the following ready before you begin registration:

- **Government-issued ID** (driver's license or passport) — for identity verification
- **Business or personal bank account** — where Amazon will deposit your payouts
- **Credit card** — to pay your monthly seller fee
- **Phone number** — for two-step verification
- **Tax information** — Social Security Number (SSN) or Employer Identification Number (EIN) for U.S. sellers

You do not need to have a registered LLC to start. You can open an account as an individual using your SSN. Many operators start this way and form a business entity later.

## Registration Walkthrough

1. Go to **sellercentral.amazon.com** and click "Sign up"
2. Create or connect an Amazon account — use a dedicated email address (not your personal shopping account)
3. Select "Professional" as your selling plan
4. Enter your business information — your legal name and address
5. Enter your credit card and bank account information
6. Complete the identity verification — Amazon will ask you to upload your ID and may request a video verification call
7. Submit your tax interview — Amazon will collect your W-9 or W-8 information at this step

## The Verification Process

After submitting, Amazon reviews your information. This typically takes 24–72 hours, though it can take up to 2 weeks in some cases. You may receive a request for additional documentation — respond promptly to avoid delays.

During this period, your account is in a pending state. You can explore Seller Central but cannot list products until verification is complete.

## Common Issues to Avoid

- **Using a personal shopping account:** Always create a separate account for your seller activities. Mixing personal and business activity creates complications.
- **Incomplete tax information:** Complete the tax interview fully. Missing this step will hold up your first payout.
- **Bank account mismatch:** The bank account you register must match the name on your seller account. Discrepancies cause payment delays.

---

**Takeaway:** Verification takes 1–3 days in most cases. Once approved, you have full access to Seller Central and can begin product research while your account finalizes.
