# Seller Central Overview

**Objective:** Navigate the Seller Central dashboard and understand the key sections you will use daily.

Seller Central is your operational hub. Every product you list, every order you receive, every payment you collect — all of it is managed here. This lesson gives you a working map of the interface so you are not guessing when it matters.

## The Dashboard

When you log in, you land on the main dashboard. It shows a summary of your recent sales, any account health alerts, and pending tasks. At the top, you will see your account health score — keep an eye on this. A green score means your account is in good standing.

## Key Sections You Will Use

**Catalog > Add Products**
This is where you list a new product. You can search for an existing listing by ASIN (Amazon's product ID) or create a new listing from scratch. For resellers, you will almost always be listing on an existing product page — not creating one.

**Inventory > Manage Inventory**
Your active listings. Here you can see every SKU you have listed, current stock levels, and whether your listing is active. If a listing is suppressed or has a quality alert, it will appear here.

**Orders > Manage Orders**
Incoming and fulfilled orders. For FBM (Fulfilled by Merchant) sellers, this is where you confirm shipments. For FBA sellers, Amazon handles fulfillment automatically.

**Inventory > Send to Amazon (FBA)**
If you are using Fulfillment by Amazon, this is where you create shipment plans and print shipping labels for your inventory.

**Reports > Payments**
Your payment history. Amazon pays out every 14 days by default, depositing directly to your bank account. This section shows your current balance, pending disbursements, and transaction history.

**Performance > Account Health**
Your account health metrics. Amazon tracks your order defect rate, late shipment rate, and policy compliance. Stay above their minimum thresholds to keep your account in good standing.

## Setting Up Your Account Before You List

Before you create your first listing, configure the following:

1. **Return settings** — Go to Settings > Return Settings and set your return address and policy. Amazon requires this.
2. **Shipping settings** — Go to Settings > Shipping Settings and configure your shipping templates (relevant for FBM sellers).
3. **Notification preferences** — Go to Settings > Notification Preferences to set up email alerts for new orders, policy warnings, and account updates.
4. **Two-step verification** — Ensure 2FA is enabled on your account. Amazon requires it and it protects your seller account from unauthorized access.

## FBA vs. FBM — A Quick Decision

At this stage, you need to choose how you will fulfill orders:

- **FBA (Fulfilled by Amazon):** You ship your inventory to Amazon's warehouse. Amazon stores, picks, packs, and ships to the customer. You pay storage fees and fulfillment fees per unit. Best for sellers who want hands-off fulfillment and Prime eligibility.
- **FBM (Fulfilled by Merchant):** You store inventory yourself and ship directly to customers when orders come in. Lower overhead, more control, but requires you to manage shipping.

For a first product with small initial inventory (under 50 units), FBM is simpler and lower cost. As you scale, FBA becomes more efficient.

---

**Takeaway:** Spend 15 minutes clicking through Seller Central before you list anything. Familiarity with the interface removes friction when you are actively operating.
