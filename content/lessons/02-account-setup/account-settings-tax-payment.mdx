# Account Settings, Tax, and Payment Setup

**Objective:** Complete all required account configurations — tax interview, disbursement schedule, and account health baselines — before listing any products.

Three configuration tasks must be completed before you can get paid: the tax interview, the deposit method, and your return address. Many new sellers skip these steps and discover them only after their first sale. Complete them now.

## Tax Interview

Amazon is required by U.S. law to collect tax information from all sellers. Navigate to **Settings → Account Info → Tax Information** and complete the tax interview.

- If you are a U.S. person selling as an individual: you will provide your SSN and complete a W-9 equivalent
- If you have an LLC or corporation: provide your EIN and business name exactly as they appear on your IRS registration

Amazon will issue a 1099-K if your sales exceed $600 in a calendar year. The tax interview determines the form type and withholding rules.

**Important:** The tax interview asks whether you are selling items that were "purchased for resale." Answer honestly — this is a legal declaration. For a standard Amazon resale operation, you are purchasing inventory from suppliers and reselling it, which is normal commercial activity.

## Deposit Methods and Disbursement Schedule

Navigate to **Settings → Account Info → Deposit Methods** and confirm your bank account is linked.

Amazon disburses funds on a 14-day rolling cycle by default. This means you will receive a payout approximately every two weeks. The payout includes all sales that have cleared minus Amazon's fees and any reserve amounts.

**Reserve Policy for New Accounts**
New seller accounts are subject to a reserve policy during the first 90 days. Amazon may withhold a portion of your balance for up to 7 days after delivery confirmation. This is normal and applies to all new accounts. The reserve percentage decreases as you build positive account history.

You can request to change to a 7-day disbursement cycle after you have established account history.

## Account Health Baseline

Before you list your first product, familiarize yourself with the three metrics Amazon monitors most closely:

| Metric | Threshold | What It Measures |
|--------|-----------|------------------|
| Order Defect Rate | < 1% | Negative feedback, A-to-Z claims, chargebacks |
| Late Shipment Rate | < 4% | Orders shipped after expected ship date |
| Valid Tracking Rate | > 95% | Orders with confirmed delivery tracking |

These numbers will be zero or undefined on a new account. Your goal is to maintain them within acceptable ranges as you begin selling. Staying inside these thresholds is not difficult — it requires shipping on time, responding to messages within 24 hours, and resolving issues before they escalate to A-to-Z claims.

Navigate to **Performance → Account Health** and bookmark this page. Check it weekly.

## Return Address

Set a return address under **Settings → Account Info → Return Address**. For FBA sellers, Amazon handles returns at their fulfillment centers, but you still need a valid return address on file. Use your home address or business address.

---

**Takeaway:** Three things before your first listing — tax interview, deposit method confirmed, return address set. Account health metrics start at zero; keep them there by shipping on time and resolving issues proactively.
