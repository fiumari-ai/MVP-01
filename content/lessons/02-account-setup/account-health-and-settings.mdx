# Account Health and Settings

**Objective:** Understand Amazon's account health system and configure your account to stay in good standing from day one.

Amazon's account health system is not complicated, but it is consequential. A suspended account means zero revenue and a difficult reinstatement process. Understanding the rules upfront prevents the most common mistakes.

## The Three Metrics That Matter Most

**Order Defect Rate (ODR)**
ODR measures the percentage of your orders that result in a negative customer experience — negative feedback, an A-to-Z claim, or a service chargeback. Amazon's threshold is under 1%. If you exceed this, your account is at risk. ODR is why product quality and accurate listing descriptions matter. A customer who feels misled files a claim. An honest listing prevents that.

**Late Shipment Rate (LSR)**
For FBM sellers, this tracks the percentage of orders you ship after the promised date. Amazon's threshold is under 4%. For FBA sellers, Amazon handles this. For FBM sellers: set realistic shipping windows, confirm shipments on time, and use tracked shipping.

**Valid Tracking Rate (VTR)**
Another metric for FBM sellers. Amazon wants to know that buyers can track their packages. Use carriers that provide tracking and upload tracking numbers in Seller Central when you confirm shipment.

## What to Configure Before You List

**Return address:** Settings > Return Settings. Enter your return address — this is where customers ship items back to you if they request a return.

**Listing quality:** When you create a listing, Amazon's listing quality dashboard will flag any missing information — no main image, incomplete descriptions, prohibited words. Address these before your listing goes live.

**Category approval:** Some Amazon categories require approval before you can list in them. Categories like Grocery, Health, Beauty, and Electronics Accessories often require what Amazon calls "ungating." This is a straightforward process of submitting invoices from an authorized supplier. We will cover this in the sourcing and listing modules.

## What NOT to Do

- **Do not try to game reviews.** Amazon's review manipulation policies are strict. Do not ask customers for positive reviews, do not offer incentives for reviews, and do not use review services. A suspended account from a review policy violation is difficult to recover.
- **Do not list counterfeit or unauthorized products.** Only list products you sourced from an authorized distributor or brand. Intellectual property complaints are one of the fastest ways to get a listing removed and account flagged.
- **Do not make extreme pricing errors.** If your price is accidentally 10x the market rate or $0, Amazon may suppress your listing or flag your account for pricing manipulation. Double-check your price before activating a listing.

## Setting Up Price Alerts

In your Seller Central account, go to **Pricing > Automate Pricing** to set up repricing rules. At the early stage, you do not need complex automation. A simple rule to stay competitive without going below your floor price is enough to prevent you from accidentally losing margin.

---

**Takeaway:** Protect your account by understanding the three core metrics (ODR, LSR, VTR) and keeping your listings honest and complete. Account health is easy to maintain when you operate correctly from the start.
