# Choosing the Right Account Type

**Objective:** Understand the difference between Individual and Professional seller accounts, and choose the right starting point.

Before you can list a single product on Amazon, you need a seller account. Amazon offers two account types: Individual and Professional. Choosing correctly at the start saves you money and avoids unnecessary complications later.

## Individual Account

The Individual account has no monthly fee. Instead, Amazon charges $0.99 per item sold, plus the standard referral fee that applies to every seller regardless of account type. This account type is appropriate if you plan to sell fewer than 40 items per month in the early testing phase and want to minimize fixed costs before you have validated your first product.

**Limitations of the Individual account:**
- You cannot use bulk listing tools
- You cannot run advertising campaigns
- You do not have access to certain product categories that require Professional status

## Professional Account

The Professional account costs $39.99 per month, but the $0.99 per-item fee is eliminated. If you are selling more than 40 items per month, the Professional plan is cheaper. More importantly, the Professional plan unlocks:

- Amazon advertising (Sponsored Products)
- Access to restricted categories
- Bulk upload tools
- More control over listing management

## Recommendation for this program

Start with an **Individual account** if you are still in the product research phase and have not placed your first inventory order. Switch to Professional once you have confirmed your first product and are ready to list. Many sellers make the switch within 30 to 60 days of starting.

---

**Takeaway:** Individual for early testing. Professional once you are ready to operate. The switch takes two minutes inside Seller Central.
