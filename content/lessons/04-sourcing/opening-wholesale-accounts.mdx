# Opening Wholesale Accounts

**Objective:** Understand the account opening process end to end so you can move from first contact to approved account efficiently.

Opening a wholesale account is not complicated, but it has a specific sequence. Suppliers have seen thousands of reseller applications. How you present yourself matters. This lesson gives you the exact process.

## Step 1 — Get Your Documents Ready

Before you contact a single supplier, have the following ready:

1. **State Resale Certificate** — Find your state's department of revenue website. Search for "resale certificate" or "seller's permit." Most states issue these online in minutes for free. This is your most important document.
2. **EIN** — Apply at irs.gov. Takes about 5 minutes. Free. Use this as your business tax ID.
3. **Business name** — You do not need a registered LLC to have a business name. Use something simple and professional. "Your Name Wholesale" or "[City] Retail Group" works fine.
4. **Amazon seller URL** — Log into Seller Central, go to your storefront, and copy the URL. Even if your store has nothing listed yet, having the link is useful.

## Step 2 — Find the Right Contact at the Supplier

Go to the supplier's website and look for:
- A "Wholesale Accounts" or "Open an Account" page — many suppliers have a dedicated application form
- A "Contact" page with a wholesale or trade sales team email
- A phone number for their sales department

Avoid the generic "info@" address. You want to reach the sales or wholesale team directly.

## Step 3 — Submit Your Application

Most suppliers have one of two processes:

**Online application form:** Fill it out completely. Do not leave fields blank. When asked for your website or business profile, put your Amazon storefront URL. Upload your resale certificate where requested.

**Email inquiry:** Some suppliers require you to email them first. Keep it professional and brief. Include:
- Who you are (your business name)
- What you want (to open a wholesale reseller account)
- Your state resale certificate as an attachment
- A brief note that you sell on Amazon

## Step 4 — Follow Up

If you hear nothing within 3–5 business days, follow up with a single brief email. A short, polite follow-up demonstrates you are a serious buyer, not someone who fires off applications and disappears.

Most legitimate suppliers respond within a week. If you hear nothing after two follow-ups, move to the next supplier on your list.

## Step 5 — Place Your First Order

Once approved, request a current price list or catalog. Review what products in your research category are available. Calculate margin using the FBA Revenue Calculator. Place a small first order — $200–$400 is a reasonable starting point. You are testing the supplier relationship and the product's sell-through rate before committing to larger inventory.

## What to Evaluate in a Supplier

When you have an approved account and a price list:
- **Price:** Does the wholesale price support at least 28% net margin on Amazon after fees?
- **Minimum order:** Is the first order minimum manageable?
- **Shipping terms:** How do they ship? Who pays freight? Is there a free shipping threshold?
- **Lead time:** How long from order to delivery? This affects your inventory planning.
- **Amazon policy:** Does the supplier allow their products to be sold on Amazon? Most do. Some have MAP (Minimum Advertised Price) policies — respect these.

---

**Takeaway:** The account opening process is: get your documents, find the right contact, apply, follow up, place a test order. The whole sequence takes 1–2 weeks from first contact to first shipment.
