# Understanding the Supplier Landscape

**Objective:** Learn the different tiers of U.S. wholesale suppliers and who to target as a new operator.

The supplier side of this model is simpler than most people expect. In the U.S., there is an entire ecosystem of wholesale distributors whose business model is to sell products in bulk to retailers — including Amazon sellers. Your job is to find the right tier of supplier for your stage and product category.

## The Three Supplier Tiers

**Tier 1 — National Distributors**
These are large national distributors like Ingram Micro, S.P. Richards, or McLane. They carry massive catalogs and sell to major retailers. They typically require high minimum orders, established business credit, and state resale certificates. These are not your starting point.

**Tier 2 — Regional and Category Wholesalers**
This is your primary target. These are mid-size wholesalers who specialize in a product category (Home & Kitchen, Health & Beauty, Pet Supplies, etc.) and sell to smaller retailers, including Amazon sellers. They often have minimums in the $100–$500 range for first orders, are willing to open accounts with new sellers, and are accessible via email outreach.

**Tier 3 — Liquidators and Closeout Buyers**
These sell excess inventory, returns, and closeout merchandise at steep discounts. The products are real and the prices can be very attractive. The challenge is inconsistency — you cannot rely on the same SKU being available regularly. For your first product, you want a product you can reorder. Liquidation is useful once you have more experience with the model.

## What Suppliers Want to See

Most Tier 2 wholesalers have a simple account opening process. They want to know you are a legitimate reseller, not a consumer trying to buy at wholesale prices. Here is what they typically require:

- **Resale certificate:** This is a state-issued certificate (also called a seller's permit or reseller license) that allows you to purchase products without paying sales tax. Getting one is free or low-cost in most states and takes a few minutes online. This is the single most important document for opening wholesale accounts.
- **Business entity or EIN:** Many suppliers accept an EIN (Employer Identification Number) from the IRS — which you can get free in minutes online. You do not need an LLC immediately, but having an EIN signals that you are operating as a business.
- **Amazon seller profile:** Some suppliers will ask for your Amazon storefront URL. Having even one or two active listings before you reach out to suppliers strengthens your application.

## The Right Expectation for First Accounts

Expect some rejections. Not every supplier will approve a brand-new account with no track record. This is normal. Experienced operators send outreach to 15–25 suppliers to get 5–10 approved accounts. You only need one to get your first product live.

Do not interpret a rejection as a sign that the model does not work. It is a filtering process. The suppliers who approve new accounts are often the ones with the most flexible terms for early-stage operators.

---

**Takeaway:** You are targeting Tier 2 category wholesalers. Get your resale certificate and EIN before you start outreach. Expect to reach out to 15+ suppliers to open your first 5 accounts.
