# Your First Supplier Outreach

**Objective:** Send your first batch of supplier outreach emails and begin opening accounts.

Most people spend too much time crafting a perfect email and not enough time sending it. Supplier outreach is a numbers game at the early stage. The template below is proven. Your job is to personalize it slightly, send it to 15–25 suppliers, and follow up with anyone who does not respond.

## The Outreach Template

Use this as your base. Adjust the bracketed fields.

---

**Subject:** Wholesale Account Inquiry — Amazon Reseller

**Body:**

Hi [Name or Wholesale Team],

My name is [Your Name] and I operate an Amazon reseller business under [Your Business Name]. I specialize in [category — e.g., Home & Kitchen / Pet Supplies / Sports accessories] and I am looking to expand my supplier relationships.

I am interested in opening a wholesale account with [Supplier Name]. I have attached my state resale certificate for [Your State] and can provide any additional documentation you require.

A few quick details about my operation:
- Selling channel: Amazon.com (storefront: [link])
- Purchasing cadence: Monthly reorders on products that perform
- First order range: $200–$500 to start, scaling with sell-through

Would you be able to send over your product catalog or current price list? I would like to evaluate a few SKUs in your [category] lineup.

Thank you,
[Your Name]
[Business Name]
[Phone]
[Email]

---

## Notes on Customization

- Replace "Amazon.com storefront" with your actual link. If you have no listings yet, write "currently setting up my Amazon storefront."
- Adjust the category to match what you are actually sourcing. Do not say "all categories" — it signals you are not a focused buyer.
- The "monthly reorders" line signals you are a repeat buyer, not a one-time order. This matters to suppliers.
- Keep it under 200 words. Buyers are busy.

## Building Your Target List

Before you start outreach, you need a list of suppliers to contact. Here is how to build one:

**Method 1 — Trade show directories:** Sites like AmericasMart, SEMA, or ASD Market Week maintain online exhibitor directories. These are real wholesale suppliers who actively seek retail buyers.

**Method 2 — Brand distributor search:** If you have identified a specific product to sell, find out who distributes it. Search "[brand name] authorized distributor" to find Tier 2 distributors who carry the brand.

**Method 3 — The Operator Path Supplier Database (Pro):** The Pro tier includes a curated database of 200+ pre-vetted suppliers organized by category, with contact info and Amazon seller policies already documented.

## Tracking Your Outreach

Use a simple spreadsheet to track every supplier you contact:
- Supplier name and website
- Date of first email
- Response received? (Y/N)
- Account status (Pending, Approved, Rejected)
- Notes (minimum order, price list received, etc.)

This prevents you from losing track of where you are with each relationship and ensures you follow up consistently.

---

**Takeaway:** Send at least 15 outreach emails in your first session. Follow up on non-responses at day 5. Expect 30–50% response rates and 20–30% approval rates. Three approved accounts is enough to begin sourcing your first product.
