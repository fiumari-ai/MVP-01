# Where Products Come From

**Objective:** Understand the product supply chain and identify which tier is best for Amazon resellers to access.

Every product on Amazon originated somewhere — a factory, a distributor, a brand owner. Understanding how products move from origin to Amazon shelf determines where you can enter the supply chain and at what cost. The resale model works because you access products closer to the origin than the average retail buyer.

## The Supply Chain Tiers

**Tier 1 — Manufacturer / Brand Owner**
The company that makes the product. They sell in minimum order quantities (MOQs) of hundreds or thousands of units. Pricing is lowest here. Access is difficult for small operators without established relationships and credit history.

**Tier 2 — Master Distributor / Wholesaler**
Companies that purchase from manufacturers in very large quantities and resell to smaller buyers. They carry thousands of SKUs across many brands. Minimum orders are typically $200–$500. This is the primary sourcing tier for Amazon resellers.

**Tier 3 — Regional Distributor / Jobber**
Smaller distributors that purchase from master distributors and serve local geographic areas. Slightly higher prices than Tier 2 but more flexible MOQs. Good for testing demand before committing to larger orders.

**Tier 4 — Retail / E-commerce**
Buying from Target, Walmart, wholesale clubs, or retail liquidators. Pricing is highest here — this is arbitrage, not wholesale. Margins are thin and inventory is inconsistent. Not a scalable primary sourcing strategy.

## Why Tier 2 Is Your Starting Point

Master distributors and wholesalers are the optimal entry point for most Amazon resellers because:

1. **Low minimums:** $200–$500 per order lets you test products without large capital commitment
2. **Catalog breadth:** One account gives you access to hundreds or thousands of SKUs
3. **Net terms:** Established accounts can often access Net 30 payment terms — you pay 30 days after receipt
4. **Repeatability:** You can reorder the same products reliably, which is essential for inventory planning
5. **No exclusivity required:** You are not locked into long-term contracts

The goal of your sourcing operation is to build accounts with 5–10 reliable wholesale distributors. Each one becomes a consistent supply channel.

## Categories of Wholesale Suppliers

Different types of wholesalers serve different product categories:

- **General line distributors:** Carry everything — housewares, personal care, food, hardware. Examples: S&S Worldwide, Topco Associates.
- **Specialty distributors:** Focus on one category — electronics, health products, sporting goods. Higher expertise, better pricing within their niche.
- **Liquidators:** Sell returned, excess, or discontinued inventory from major retailers at deep discounts. Unpredictable SKU mix but very low cost. Not suitable as a primary channel.
- **Brand-authorized distributors:** Licensed to sell a specific brand. More reliable supply and often access to restricted products.

## Domestic vs. International Sourcing

For your first product, source domestically — from a U.S.-based distributor. Domestic sourcing means:
- Short lead times (3–7 days vs. 30–60 days from overseas)
- No import tariffs or customs delays
- Established product quality expectations
- Easier returns and reorders

International sourcing (primarily from China via Alibaba) is a strategy for creating private label products — a more complex and capital-intensive approach that is outside the scope of this program.

---

**Takeaway:** Tier 2 wholesale distributors are your starting point. One distributor account = access to hundreds of SKUs. Build 5–10 distributor relationships to create a diversified, repeatable supply chain.
