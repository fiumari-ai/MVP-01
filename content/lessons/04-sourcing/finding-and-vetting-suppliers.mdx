# Finding and Vetting Suppliers

**Objective:** Locate legitimate wholesale distributors, open accounts, and verify that you are buying from an authorized supply source.

Finding suppliers is not difficult once you know where to look. The harder part is verifying that a supplier is legitimate — that they are actually authorized to sell the brands you want to carry and that their pricing is competitive.

## Where to Find Wholesale Distributors

**Industry trade directories:**
- Worldwide Brands (worldwidebrands.com) — paid directory of verified wholesalers
- Wholesale Central (wholesalecentral.com) — free directory, less curated
- Thomas Net (thomasnet.com) — industrial and B2B suppliers

**Trade shows:**
ASD Market Week (Las Vegas, twice yearly) is the largest general merchandise trade show in the U.S. Walking the floor gives you access to hundreds of distributors in one place and lets you negotiate accounts in person. OFFPRICE is another trade show focused on off-price and closeout merchandise.

**Brand websites:**
Most brand websites have a "Where to Buy" or "Become a Retailer" section. Contact the brand directly and ask who their authorized distributors are for your region.

**Competitor investigation:**
Buy a competitor's product on Amazon and check the packaging. Distributors are sometimes listed on inner packaging or packing slips. You can also contact the brand directly and tell them you are an Amazon seller looking to stock their products — they will typically refer you to their authorized channels.

**Direct Google search:**
Search for "[product category] wholesale distributor USA" or "[brand name] authorized distributor." This surfaces both general and specialty distributors.

## Opening a Wholesale Account

Most legitimate wholesalers require:
1. **Business entity:** An LLC or sole proprietorship is sufficient. You do not need a formal corporation.
2. **EIN or SSN:** For tax purposes (you will fill out a W-9)
3. **Resale certificate / sales tax exemption:** Most states allow you to purchase inventory for resale without paying sales tax. Apply for a resale certificate through your state's tax authority.
4. **A real website or Amazon store link:** Some distributors require proof of legitimate business operations

The application process varies. Some wholesalers approve accounts immediately online. Others require a phone call or approval review that takes 1–3 business days.

## Vetting a Supplier

Before placing your first order, verify three things:

**1. Authorization**
Is this distributor authorized to sell the brand? Unauthorized sellers (also called "gray market") can source products that are counterfeit, diverted, or missing warranties. If a brand is sold on Amazon and requires authorization, you need to be buying from an authorized source to avoid intellectual property complaints.

How to verify: Contact the brand's sales team and ask if [distributor name] is an authorized reseller of their products. Most brands will confirm or deny over email.

**2. Pricing competitiveness**
Request a price list (usually called a "price sheet" or "catalog") and compare the distributor's cost against the current Amazon selling price. Calculate your margin using Amazon's FBA Revenue Calculator. If the margin is below 15% after fees, the pricing is not competitive.

**3. Minimum order and payment terms**
Understand the minimum order amount, whether they offer Net 30 terms, and their return/damage policy. New accounts typically require prepayment. Net 30 terms are usually available after 2–3 successful orders.

---

**Takeaway:** Start with trade directories and brand referrals. Verify authorization before buying. Good distributors are transparent about their terms, have real catalogs, and are willing to confirm their brand relationships.
