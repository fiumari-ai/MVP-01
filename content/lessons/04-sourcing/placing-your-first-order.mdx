# Placing Your First Order

**Objective:** Calculate your initial order quantity, meet FBA prep requirements, and get your first inventory shipment into Amazon's fulfillment network.

Your first order should be small — the goal is to validate that the product sells at your projected price, that the supply chain works, and that your margin calculations were correct. You are not trying to maximize profit on the first order. You are buying information.

## How Much to Order

A reliable formula for first-order quantity:

**Target sell-through in 30–45 days at your estimated velocity**

Example: If the product's BSR suggests it sells 200 units per month across all competing sellers, and there are 10 sellers competing, each seller moves approximately 20 units/month. Order 20–30 units as your test batch.

This approach limits your capital at risk to one month of inventory. If the product sells faster than expected, you reorder. If it sells slower, you have not over-committed.

**First order budget recommendation:** $200–$500 in product cost. This is enough to generate real sales data without material financial risk.

## Understanding FBA Requirements

Before your order arrives from your supplier, you need to create a shipment in Amazon Seller Central. Navigate to **Inventory → Add a Product** and create a listing for your product using its existing ASIN (assuming you are selling a product that is already on Amazon).

Once the listing is created, go to **Inventory → Manage FBA Inventory → Send/Replenish Inventory** to create a shipping plan.

Amazon will tell you:
- Which fulfillment center(s) to ship to
- Label requirements (each unit needs an Amazon FNSKU barcode label)
- Box content requirements

**FNSKU Labels**
Every unit sent to Amazon FBA needs an FNSKU label — a unique identifier that ties the unit to your seller account. You can have your supplier apply these labels (some charge a small fee), or you can apply them yourself before shipping to Amazon.

Print FNSKU labels from Seller Central under **Inventory → Manage FBA Inventory → Print Item Labels**.

## FBA Prep Standards

Amazon has specific packaging and prep requirements depending on the product type:

- **Bagged items:** Must be sealed and labeled. Amazon provides prep guidelines for each ASIN.
- **Poly bags:** Required for products that could be considered a suffocation hazard (bags larger than 5 inches)
- **Bubble wrap:** Required for fragile items
- **Sets:** Multi-unit sets must be labeled "Sold as Set" and packaged together

Non-compliant shipments will be rejected at the fulfillment center, and Amazon charges preparation fees to fix non-compliant items at their facility.

When in doubt: over-prep, don't under-prep. The cost of a damaged unit returned to you is higher than the cost of extra packaging.

## Shipping to Amazon

Once your shipping plan is created in Seller Central:

1. Package your inventory according to Amazon's specifications
2. Apply FNSKU labels to each unit
3. Apply Amazon's box labels to each carton
4. Ship via your preferred carrier (UPS, FedEx, USPS). Small parcel shipments work for most first-time orders.

After your carrier picks up the shipment, enter your tracking number in Seller Central. Amazon typically receives and processes FBA shipments within 3–7 business days. Your inventory will appear as "available" in Seller Central once it has been received and processed.

## First Listing Health Check

Before your inventory is received, verify your listing is correct:
- Price is set at or slightly above the current Buy Box price
- You are using "Fulfilled by Amazon" (FBA) on the offer
- Your listing condition is "New"
- Handling time is set to 1 day (FBA handles this automatically)

---

**Takeaway:** First order: 20–30 units, $200–$500 budget. Create the listing and shipping plan before your order ships. Apply FNSKU labels to every unit. Your goal is validation, not volume.
