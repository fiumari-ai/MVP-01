# Supplier Outreach — Advanced Techniques

**Objective:** Build supplier relationships that go beyond transactional and give you structural advantages over other resellers.

Basic outreach gets you an account. Advanced relationship building gets you priority access, early product alerts, and negotiated pricing. As your operation grows, supplier relationships become one of your primary competitive advantages.

## Beyond the Initial Account Opening

Once you have an approved account and have placed your first order, the relationship is just beginning. Most resellers treat suppliers as a catalog to order from. Operators who grow fastest treat suppliers as business partners.

**Check in after your first order.** Send a brief email to your wholesale rep: "Just received the shipment — everything looks good. Looking forward to the next order." This single act puts you in the top 20% of their reseller accounts, because most buyers never follow up.

**Share what's selling.** If a product from their catalog is performing well on Amazon, tell them. "Your SKU X has been moving 40 units a month — do you have volume pricing at that level?" This creates mutual value and often opens a pricing conversation.

## Negotiating Better Terms Over Time

After 3–4 consistent orders, you have leverage to negotiate:

**Volume pricing:** Most wholesale suppliers have tiered pricing. Ask: "What does your pricing look like at [next tier quantity]?" Even a 3% reduction per unit can meaningfully improve your margin on a fast-moving product.

**Freight terms:** Ask about free shipping thresholds. "At what order size do you offer free freight?" Many suppliers have a threshold in the $500–$1,000 range.

**Early access to new products:** Ask your rep to add you to their "new arrivals" notification list. Being the first seller on a new product listing gives you a window of reduced competition.

**Payment terms:** Net-15 or Net-30 terms (pay 15–30 days after receiving goods) improve your cash flow. This is typically available after you have established 6+ months of reliable order history.

## Dealing with Stock Shortages

Suppliers run out of popular products. When they do, they allocate remaining stock to their best accounts first. Being a reliable, consistent buyer puts you in that priority tier.

If a supplier notifies you of limited stock on a product you depend on:
1. Order immediately up to your realistic selling capacity
2. Search the database or your other supplier relationships for an alternative source
3. Adjust your pricing slightly upward to slow sell-through if you cannot restock quickly (this prevents stockout while protecting your BSR)

## Building a Supplier Network, Not a Single Dependency

Goal for your first 6 months: 5–10 approved wholesale accounts across multiple categories. This prevents any single supplier relationship from being a bottleneck. If one supplier goes out of stock, you have alternatives. If one raises prices, you have leverage to negotiate or switch.

---

**Takeaway:** Treat your supplier relationships as partnerships, not transactions. Consistent communication, reliable ordering, and sharing performance data builds the kind of relationship that gives you structural advantages over other resellers.
