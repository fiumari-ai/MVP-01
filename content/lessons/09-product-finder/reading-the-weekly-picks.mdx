# Reading the Weekly Picks

**Objective:** Understand what the weekly product picks show you and how to evaluate them against your situation.

New picks are added every Monday. Each pick has passed a five-point internal filter before being added. This lesson explains every data field so you can evaluate picks in minutes, not hours.

## The Data Fields

**Product Name and Category**
The general product type and the Amazon category it belongs to. You do not need to know the exact brand or ASIN at this point — the category tells you which supplier vertical to look in.

**Demand Score (0–100)**
A composite score based on current BSR, BSR trend over 90 days, and estimated monthly sales volume. Higher is better. Scores above 70 indicate strong, consistent demand. Scores between 50–70 indicate solid demand with some volatility. Below 50 means demand is moderate — still viable, but requires more precision on pricing and sourcing.

**Estimated Margin**
The margin estimate assumes a reasonable wholesale price for that product category and current Amazon selling prices. This is an estimate, not a guarantee — your actual margin depends on what cost you can negotiate with your specific supplier. Use this as a directional signal, not a final number.

**Average Price**
The current average selling price across active FBA offers on that product. This helps you assess whether the price range fits your capital situation.

**Competition Level (Low / Medium / High)**
A summary of how many competing FBA sellers are active on this listing and how aggressive their pricing is:
- **Low:** Under 8 FBA sellers, pricing relatively stable
- **Medium:** 8–20 FBA sellers, some price pressure
- **High:** 20+ FBA sellers or the brand is dominant — harder for new operators to compete

**Why Now**
A brief note on why this product is appearing in this week's picks — a seasonal opportunity, a supplier we have confirmed has open accounts, a recent BSR trend that indicates growing demand, or a competitor who recently stocked out.

**Source Hint**
A directional note on where to look for this product in the supplier database. Not a specific supplier, but a category or supplier type to prioritize.

## How to Evaluate a Pick Against Your Situation

Ask three questions about each pick:

1. **Can I source it?** Does my existing supplier network or the database have suppliers in this category at a price supporting the estimated margin?

2. **Can I afford the first order?** At the average selling price and typical wholesale margins, what would a 20–30 unit first order cost? Does that fit your current capital?

3. **Do I have time to act this week?** Product Finder picks are timely. A "Low competition" pick will not stay that way indefinitely. If you are going to pursue it, begin outreach within 2–3 days of it appearing.

---

**Takeaway:** Scan the weekly picks on Monday. Shortlist picks with Demand Score 65+, Low to Medium competition, and a source hint that matches your supplier access. Pursue your top 1–2 picks before the next drop.
